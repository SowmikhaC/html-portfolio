The Conjuring

The Conjuring is a supernatural horror film series inspired by real-life paranormal investigations conducted by Ed and Lorraine Warren, two famous demonologists. The first movie, The Conjuring (2013), directed by James Wan, became a massive success for its chilling atmosphere and true-story basis.

The story revolves around the Perron family, who move into a farmhouse in Rhode Island, only to experience terrifying supernatural events. As the haunting grows stronger, they call Ed and Lorraine Warren for help. The Warrens uncover a dark and powerful presence tied to the house’s history — leading to one of the most intense exorcisms ever portrayed on screen.

The success of the first film led to an entire “Conjuring Universe”, including movies like Annabelle, The Nun, and The Curse of La Llorona — all connected by the Warrens’ investigations. The series is known for its eerie silence, sudden scares, emotional depth, and realistic storytelling, making it one of the most iconic horror franchises in modern cinema.
